# AI Governance Policy

## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish governance, security, compliance, and risk management requirements for the adoption and use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) technologies within an enterprise environment.

This policy is intended to support secure, responsible, and compliant AI adoption while protecting organizational assets, data, and business operations.

---

# Scope

This policy applies to:

- Employees
- Contractors
- Third-party vendors
- Consultants
- Business units utilizing AI technologies

The policy covers:

- Large Language Models (LLMs)
- AI Assistants
- Generative AI Platforms
- AI-Powered SaaS Applications
- Custom AI Applications
- Third-Party AI Services

---

# Approved AI Services

Only approved AI platforms may be used for business purposes.

Examples include:

- ChatGPT Enterprise
- Microsoft Copilot
- Microsoft Security Copilot
- Approved internal AI solutions

The use of unapproved AI services may result in disciplinary action and security review.

---

# Data Handling Requirements

## Restricted Data

The following information must not be submitted to public AI platforms:

- Customer data
- Personally Identifiable Information (PII)
- Financial records
- Intellectual property
- Legal documents
- Authentication credentials
- Confidential business information

---

## Approved Data Usage

Users may submit:

- Public information
- Approved training materials
- Sanitized business content
- Non-sensitive documentation

All data submitted to AI platforms must comply with organizational data classification policies.

---

# Human Oversight Requirements

AI systems must not independently perform security-critical or business-critical actions without human approval.

Examples include:

- Account suspension
- User termination
- Financial approvals
- Security containment actions
- Policy exceptions

Human review must occur before execution of high-risk actions.

---

# AI Risk Assessments

AI systems must undergo risk assessments before deployment.

Assessments should evaluate:

- Data exposure risks
- Prompt injection risks
- Third-party dependencies
- Model misuse scenarios
- Compliance requirements
- Operational impacts

Risk assessments should be reviewed annually or following major system changes.

---

# Monitoring Requirements

Organizations must implement monitoring controls that include:

- Audit logging
- User activity monitoring
- API monitoring
- Prompt monitoring
- Security event collection
- Incident detection

Logs should be retained according to enterprise retention requirements.

---

# Vendor Risk Management

Third-party AI providers must undergo security review before approval.

Reviews should evaluate:

- Security controls
- Privacy practices
- Data handling procedures
- Compliance certifications
- Incident response capabilities

Vendor risk reviews should be performed periodically.

---

# Incident Reporting

Security incidents involving AI systems must be reported immediately.

Examples include:

- Prompt injection attempts
- Unauthorized access
- Data leakage
- AI abuse
- Suspicious outputs
- Third-party service compromise

All incidents should follow existing enterprise incident response procedures.

---

# Compliance Requirements

AI systems must comply with applicable:

- Corporate policies
- Regulatory requirements
- Industry standards
- Security frameworks

Examples include:

- NIST Cybersecurity Framework
- NIST AI RMF
- GDPR
- HIPAA
- SOC 2
- PCI-DSS

---

# Roles and Responsibilities

## Security Team

Responsible for:

- Risk assessments
- Security reviews
- Incident response
- Monitoring controls

---

## Business Owners

Responsible for:

- Appropriate AI usage
- Compliance with policy requirements
- Risk acceptance decisions

---

## End Users

Responsible for:

- Following approved AI usage guidelines
- Protecting sensitive information
- Reporting suspicious activity

---

# Policy Review

This policy should be reviewed annually and updated as AI technologies, threats, and regulatory requirements evolve.

---

# Conclusion

Effective AI governance requires a combination of security controls, risk management, monitoring, compliance oversight, and human accountability. Organizations should maintain continuous governance processes to support secure AI adoption.
