# Executive Summary

## Purpose

Organizations are rapidly adopting Artificial Intelligence (AI) technologies to improve productivity, automate workflows, enhance decision-making, and support business operations.

---

## Objectives

The objectives of this framework are:

- Identify AI-specific threats and attack vectors
- Assess potential business and security impacts
- Establish governance requirements for AI usage
- Recommend security controls for AI-enabled systems
- Align security practices with recognized frameworks such as NIST AI RMF

---

## Scope

This assessment focuses on:

- Enterprise AI assistants
- Large Language Models (LLMs)
- AI-powered productivity tools
- Third-party AI services integrated into business environments

---

## Key Findings

The highest-risk areas identified include:

- Prompt Injection Attacks
- Sensitive Data Exposure
- Excessive AI Permissions
- Third-Party AI Dependencies
- Insider Misuse
- AI-Generated Decision Errors

---

## Conclusion

Organizations adopting AI should implement:

- Strong governance
- Human oversight
- Monitoring and logging
- Access controls
- Risk management processes

to reduce exposure and securely enable AI adoption.
